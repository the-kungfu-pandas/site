Interview

What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

I am the GIS/Eng Apps Team Manager, I am the Product Owner for many applications, but my main focus is for the Geographic Information Systems (GIS) applications, Electricity, Gas and Telecommunications. I have a team of 6 direct reports and with 3 contractors, I am responsible for the planning, design, build and operation of the GIS/Eng Applications.

What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other ITprofessionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

We interact with the Business Owner of the applications we support. We work with the business owner to prioritise the work. We develop and implement solutions that meet business requirements, sometimes we need to interact with other IT teams to implement change. This could be other application development teams or Infrastructure support teams when it comes to uplifting new hardware which is mainly virtual.

Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

As the manager and product owner of many applications, I spend a lot of my time in meetings discussing how we can support current and future projects that require our assistance. I am directly involved in Project Control Boards (PCBs) where I am the product owner. This year we are running a number of upgrade projects.

What aspect of their position is most challenging?

Get resources to be available to support the number of activities going on at once. The onboarding process to bring on contractors can be difficult. I also like to retain the IP within our team rather than contractors gaining IP so I tend to put my direct reports on the projects and backfill them. This can also be difficult finding the right skillset to backfill.
